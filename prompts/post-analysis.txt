```md
Write two detailed markdown files inside the current package’s `markdowns/` folder.

---

# 1. `SESSION_SUMMARY.md`

Write a detailed chronological summary of this session.

Include:
- task goals,
- package-generation workflow,
- important design decisions,
- benchmark investigations,
- testcase-generation discussions,
- wrong-solution analysis,
- subtask-separation reasoning,
- refinement architecture evolution,
- retrieval/routing discussions,
- benchmark/TLE methodology discussions,
- package-structure changes,
- and important operational discoveries.

The summary should:
- preserve technical reasoning,
- explain why important decisions were made,
- and remain understandable to someone who did not participate in the session.

Do not merely list commits/changes.
Capture the reasoning and evolution of the session.

---

# 2. `SKILL_CORRECTIONS.md`

Write a highly detailed skill-refinement and correction document.

Purpose:
- accumulate reusable lessons from this session,
- explain what the skill previously lacked or handled poorly,
- and document what should be improved in future skill updates.

Important:
- include concrete specifics from this task/problem/session,
- because future refinement may happen long after this session,
- and future readers may not know the original context.

The document should:
- explain the task context clearly,
- explain the observed failure or weakness,
- explain why it mattered,
- explain the corrected methodology,
- explain the intended canonical owner,
- and explain whether the issue was:
  - missing knowledge,
  - retrieval failure,
  - checklist failure,
  - template failure,
  - execution drift,
  - or architecture/routing issue.

Focus especially on:
- testcase methodology,
- benchmark methodology,
- subtask separation,
- weak tests accidentally allowing slow solutions,
- benchmarking optimized/TLE variants,
- generator decomposition,
- scorer semantics,
- constraints synchronization,
- refinement workflow evolution,
- retrieval-quality issues,
- and operational conventions.

This document should be as detailed as reasonably possible.
It is intended to support future batched skill updates across multiple sessions.

---

Requirements:
- Use Markdown with LaTeX formatting conventions.
- Keep explanations operational and evidence-based.
- Do not regenerate packages/tests/code unless necessary for the markdown generation itself.
- Preserve canonical ownership philosophy; this is documentation, not architecture redesign.
```
